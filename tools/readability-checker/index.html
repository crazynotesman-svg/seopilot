    Why Readability Matters for SEO

    Google's algorithms increasingly prioritize user experience signals — and readability is a big part of that. Content that's easy to read keeps visitors on your page longer, reduces bounce rates, and increases the likelihood of social shares and backlinks. Readable content also performs better with voice search, as it more closely matches natural language queries.

    Understanding the Scores

    • Flesch Reading Ease (0–100) — Higher is easier to read. 90–100: Very Easy (5th grade). 60–70: Standard (8th–9th grade, ideal for most blogs). 0–30: Very Difficult (college graduate level).
    • Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level — The U.S. school grade level needed to understand the text. Aim for grade 7–9 for general audiences.
    • Average Sentence Length — Shorter sentences (under 20 words) are easier to process. Mix short and medium sentences for rhythm.
    • Passive Voice — Too much passive voice makes writing feel distant and bureaucratic. Keep it under 15%.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is a good Flesch Reading Ease score for blog content?
    For general blog content, aim for a Flesch Reading Ease score of 60–70. This corresponds to an 8th–9th grade reading level, which is comfortable for most adult readers online. Technical or academic content may naturally score lower.
    How is the Flesch score calculated?
    The formula is: 206.835 − 1.015 × (words/sentences) − 84.6 × (syllables/words). It considers average sentence length and average syllables per word. Shorter sentences and words with fewer syllables produce higher (better) scores.
    Does readability directly affect Google rankings?
    Google has not confirmed readability as a direct ranking factor. However, readable content indirectly improves rankings by increasing dwell time, reducing bounce rate, and generating more backlinks and shares — all of which are confirmed ranking signals.

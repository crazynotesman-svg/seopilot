Why Meta Tags Matter

    
Meta tags are invisible HTML elements that tell search engines and social platforms what your page is about. Good meta tags improve your click-through rate from search results and make your content look professional when shared on social media. Without them, platforms will auto-generate previews that may not accurately represent your content.


    
Meta Tags Explained

    
    
      
  • Title Tag — The most important on-page SEO element. Appears as the clickable link in search results.
    • 
      
  • Meta Description — The snippet below your title in search results. Doesn't directly affect rankings but significantly impacts CTR.
    • 
      
  • Open Graph Tags — Control how your page looks when shared on Facebook, LinkedIn, Slack, and most messaging apps.
    • 
      
  • Twitter Cards — Similar to Open Graph but specifically for Twitter/X sharing.
    • 
      
  • Canonical URL — Tells search engines which URL is the primary version when duplicate content exists.
    • 
      
  • Robots Meta — Controls whether search engines should index the page and follow its links.

Frequently Asked Questions

    

      
Do meta tags still matter for SEO in 2026?

      
Yes. While meta keywords are largely ignored by Google, title tags and meta descriptions remain critical. Title tags are a direct ranking factor, and well-written descriptions improve click-through rates, which indirectly helps rankings.

    

    

      
What's the difference between OG tags and Twitter Cards?

      
Open Graph tags are used by Facebook, LinkedIn, and most social platforms. Twitter has its own card system but falls back to OG tags if Twitter-specific tags aren't present. It's best to include both.

    

    

      
What size should my OG image be?

      
The recommended size is 1200x630 pixels with an aspect ratio of 1.91:1. This ensures your image looks good on both desktop and mobile feeds across all platforms.
